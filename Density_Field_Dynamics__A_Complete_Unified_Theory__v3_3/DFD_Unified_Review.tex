\begin{abstract}
Density Field Dynamics (\DFD) is a scalar refractive-index theory of gravity 
defined by the postulate that spacetime is flat but permeated by a scalar field 
$\psi(\mathbf{x},t)$ establishing an optical refractive index $n = e^\psi$. 
Light propagates according to the eikonal of the optical metric 
$d\tilde{s}^2 = -c^2 dt^2/n^2 + d\mathbf{x}^2$, while matter responds to the 
effective potential $\Phi = -c^2\psi/2$. This framework has an \textbf{optical scalar sector} 
$\psi$ that governs clock rates, refraction, and quasi-static dynamics, together with a 
\textbf{transverse-traceless radiative sector} $h^{\rm TT}_{ij}$ for gravitational waves,
derived as the spin-2 irreducible component of the same zero-mode parent tensor on
$\mathbb{C}P^2 \times S^3$ whose trace yields $\psi$~\cite{AlcockConstitutive2026}. 
It reproduces all classic tests of general relativity in the weak-field limit 
($\gamma = \beta = 1$, all PPN parameters matching \GR), gravitational waves 
at speed $c$ with two tensor polarizations, and MOND-like phenomenology at 
galactic scales through a nonlinear crossover function $\mu(x) = x/(1+x)$ 
and scale $a_* = 2\sqrt{\alpha}\,cH_0$, \textbf{both derived from $S^3$ topology} (Appendix~\ref{app:mu-derivation}). A dedicated model-independent SPARC shape analysis further finds $n_{\rm opt}=1.15\pm0.12$ (95\% CI $[1.00,1.50]$) in the family $\mu_n(x)=x/(1+x^n)^{1/n}$, with DFD's $n=1$ inside the confidence region and Standard MOND's $n=2$ strongly disfavored.

with Toeplitz truncation at $k_{\max} = 60$. The derivation is \textbf{convention-locked}: a forced binary fork between regular-module 
and fermion-rep microsectors is resolved by a no-hidden-knobs policy, with the surviving branch matching experiment at sub-ppm level. 
\textbf{Verified by lattice Monte Carlo} (L6--L16; 9/10 at L16 with $p < 0.01$, mean $+1.1\%$);

(Appendix~\ref{app:complete-params}). The 5/3 GUT normalization factor is \textbf{derived}, not assumed. 
Agreement with $\sin^2\theta_W^{\rm exp} = 0.23122$: \textbf{0.2\%} (tree-level vs $\overline{\rm MS}$);

and the unique proper-time$\to\overline{\rm MS}$ matching factor $\sqrt{4\pi}$ (Appendix~\ref{app:complete-params}). 
Agreement with PDG 2024 ($0.1180 \pm 0.0009$): \textbf{$0.8\sigma$};

(observed: 246.22 GeV, \textbf{0.05\% error})---the 17 orders of magnitude are topological, not fine-tuned.

achieves \textbf{1.42\% mean error}. Prefactors computed via explicit Yukawa operator: CP$^2$ kernels 
$K_d = J_3$, $K_u = I_4$ fixed by symmetry (Lemma~\ref{lem:kernel}), QCD factors from $b_0 = 7$, 
generation operator $G = {\rm diag}(2/3,1,1)$ derived from primed microsector trace (Theorem~\ref{thm:G-derived}, Appendix~\ref{app:microsector});

$(\lambda, A, \bar{\rho}, \bar{\eta}) = (31, 108, 19, 49) \times \alpha$ with 0.55\% mean agreement, where integers 
arise from $\mathbb{C}P^2$ line bundle cohomology (Appendix~\ref{app:complete-params}). Selection rule identifying 
which cohomologies govern each parameter remains open. PMNS from tribimaximal base + charged lepton corrections;

with $J \neq 0$ (CKM CP preserved). All-orders: CP anomaly vanishes because the mapping torus has even dimension (8), 
forcing $\eta = 0$ by spectral symmetry (Appendix~\ref{app:strongcp_selection_rule}). \textbf{No axion required};

via Gaussian mode integration on the finite-dimensional microsector (Appendix~\ref{app:O_alpha57_clean}): the exponent 57 is topologically forced by primed-determinant 
scaling on the finite microsector state space; the per-mode suppression factor $\alpha$ follows from uniform gauge normalization with exact eigenvalue cancellation (Lemma~O.4); the identification with the physical hierarchy uses the finite dimensionality of the microsector to eliminate all UV ambiguities (Lemma~O.5). 
This predicts $H_0 = 72.09$ km/s/Mpc, matching JWST distance-ladder measurements (SH0ES JWST combined: $72.6 \pm 2.0$ km/s/Mpc, $0.3\sigma$ agreement) 
but disagreeing with Planck CMB-inferred $H_0 = 67.4 \pm 0.5$ km/s/Mpc at $9.4\sigma$ (Planck statistical uncertainty)---the ``Hubble tension'' is interpreted as a $\psi$-screen optical bias;
%
(7)~\textbf{UVCS test:} Ly-$\alpha$/O~VI asymmetry ratio $R = \Gamma \times (\sigma_{\rm OVI}/\sigma_{\rm Ly\alpha})^2$ with 
$\Gamma_{\rm obs} = 4.4 \pm 0.9$ matching DFD's double-transit prediction $\Gamma = 4$ ($0.4\sigma$); standard physics predicts $\Gamma = 1$;

peak location $\ell_1 = 220$ from $\psi$-lensing with $\Delta\psi = 0.30$;

distance ratios---the ``accelerating expansion'' is reinterpreted as an optical effect requiring no dark energy;

Branch B exponents $k = \alpha^{-3/11}$, $r = \alpha^{-7/20}$ from microsector integers; absolute scale 
$m_3 = (14/13)\pi M_P\alpha^{14}$ from finite-$d$ priming. Predictions: $\Delta m^2_{21} = 7.48 \times 10^{-5}$ eV$^2$, 
$\Delta m^2_{31} = 2.51 \times 10^{-3}$ eV$^2$ (NuFIT 6.0: $\chi^2 = 0.025$, $p = 0.99$); 
$\sum m_\nu = 61.4$ meV; combined hierarchy exponent $k^2r^2 = \alpha^{-137/110}$ (numerator is $\alpha^{-1}$);

derived from the same $S^3$ saturation-union composition law that fixed $\mu(x)$. Key results: (i) temporal deviation

quadratic identification gives $w \to 1/2$ (not dust). Full $P(k)$ matching is a program item, not a theorem.

reconstruct $\Delta\psi_{\rm screen}$ independently; a third estimator (duality) serves as a metric-consistency check

redshift bins provides a quantitative falsifier. No dynamical assumption about $\mu(x)$ or growth required.

An intentional $2\omega$ modulation search could reach $|\lambda - 1| \sim 10^{-14}$ with existing apparatus;

for the initial-boundary value problem on bounded domains. Energy estimates with Gronwall bound ensure stability. 
Finite speed of propagation guarantees causality;

(16)~\textbf{Late-time observations (Sec.~\ref{sec:obs-status}):} DES Y3 Weyl potential 2--3$\sigma$ shallower at low $z$ 
(supportive); DESI DR2 $w(z) \neq -1$ hints (consistent with $\psi$-screen); wide binaries active/contested; 
$E_G$ and KiDS-Legacy show mild tension.
