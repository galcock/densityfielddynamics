# DFD SBIR/STTR OPPORTUNITY ANALYSIS AND PROPOSAL STRATEGY

**Date:** March 2026
**Prepared for:** DFD Defense Applications Division

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The DFD patent portfolio aligns with numerous active and recurring DoD SBIR/STTR topic areas across all service branches and DARPA. This document identifies the highest-probability opportunities, maps each to specific DFD patents, provides proposal abstracts, and recommends a prioritized pursuit strategy. The analysis is based on established DoD research priority areas, known program office interests, and the recurring SBIR/STTR topic categories that appear in each solicitation cycle.

**Recommendation:** Pursue 6-8 SBIR Phase I proposals across 3-4 agencies in the next two solicitation cycles, with a primary focus on DARPA DSO (novel physics), Navy (submarine stealth and undersea comms), and Army/PEO IEW&S (PNT).

## 1. DARPA OPPORTUNITIES

### 1.1 Defense Sciences Office (DSO) -- Novel Physical Phenomena

**Recurring Topic Area:** DSO regularly solicits proposals for novel physical phenomena with defense applications, including new propulsion concepts, novel sensing modalities, and fundamental physics breakthroughs.

**DFD Alignment:** The psi-field itself -- artificial generation and control of a scalar refractive field -- is exactly the type of novel physical phenomenon DSO funds.

**Proposal Concept:** "Experimental Demonstration of Controllable Scalar Refractive Fields for Defense Applications"

- **Phase I ($250K, 12 months):** Design and construct a parametric psi-resonator (Patent 2). Demonstrate measurable psi-field generation and characterize field amplitude, spatial extent, and controllability. Develop detection/measurement instrumentation.
- **Phase II ($1M, 24 months):** Scale resonator to produce macroscopic refractive-index modulation. Demonstrate beam-steering with metamaterial amplifier panel. Quantify drag reduction in laboratory flow experiment.
- **Relevant Patents:** 2 (primary), 1, all others (downstream applications)
- **Probability of Fit:** HIGH -- DSO exists to fund paradigm-shifting physics

### 1.2 Tactical Technology Office (TTO) -- Advanced Propulsion and Vehicle Concepts

**Recurring Topic Area:** TTO solicits concepts for advanced vehicle performance including drag reduction, propulsion efficiency, and trans-medium operation.

**DFD Alignment:** Patent 1 directly addresses drag reduction for maritime, aerospace, and trans-medium vehicles.

**Proposal Concept:** "Scalar Refractive Field Drag Reduction for Next-Generation Undersea and Trans-Medium Vehicles"

- **Phase I:** Theoretical and computational analysis of psi-field bubble formation around representative vehicle geometries. CFD modeling of drag reduction at operationally relevant Reynolds numbers. Preliminary resonator design for vehicle integration.
- **Phase II:** Water-tunnel demonstration of psi-field drag reduction on scale model. Measure drag coefficient, turbulence suppression, and acoustic signature reduction.
- **Relevant Patents:** 1, 2
- **Probability of Fit:** HIGH -- directly addresses DARPA vehicle programs

### 1.3 Microsystems Technology Office (MTO) -- Quantum Technologies

**Recurring Topic Area:** MTO funds advances in quantum computing, quantum sensing, and quantum networking, including novel approaches to decoherence mitigation.

**DFD Alignment:** Patent 3 provides a fundamentally new approach to quantum error suppression through continuous psi-field stabilization.

**Proposal Concept:** "Scalar Refractive Field Quantum Coherence Stabilization for Fault-Tolerant Quantum Processors"

- **Phase I:** Theoretical modeling of psi-field coupling to superconducting qubit systems. Design of hybrid EM-psi control architecture. Simulation of decoherence suppression performance.
- **Phase II:** Construct psi-field generator integrated with superconducting qubit testbed. Measure coherence time extension. Demonstrate error suppression below conventional digital correction baseline.
- **Relevant Patents:** 3, 2, 10
- **Probability of Fit:** MEDIUM-HIGH -- novel approach to a well-funded problem area

### 1.4 Strategic Technology Office (STO) -- Resilient PNT and Communications

**Recurring Topic Area:** STO funds resilient positioning, navigation, timing, and communications technologies for contested environments.

**DFD Alignment:** Patents 5, 8, and 9 provide fundamentally new PNT and communications architectures immune to EM-based threats.

**Proposal Concept:** "Geometry-Locked Scalar Field Navigation and Communications for GNSS-Denied Environments"

- **Phase I:** Design psi-PNT receiver architecture. Model position accuracy vs. emitter geometry and range. Develop spoof-rejection algorithm based on curvature tensor verification.
- **Phase II:** Construct 3-emitter psi-PNT constellation at laboratory/building scale. Demonstrate position determination and spoof rejection.
- **Relevant Patents:** 5, 8, 9
- **Probability of Fit:** HIGH -- directly addresses top DoD capability gap

### 1.5 Information Innovation Office (I2O) -- Secure Communications

**Recurring Topic Area:** I2O funds advances in secure, resilient communications including anti-jam, anti-spoof, and LPI/LPD capabilities.

**Proposal Concept:** "Physically Unjammable Communication via Scalar Refractive Field Corridors"

- **Phase I:** Theoretical channel capacity analysis for psi-corridor communication. Design modulation and demodulation architecture. Analyze geometry-locked authentication performance.
- **Phase II:** Laboratory demonstration of psi-communication link with symbol transmission and geometry-locked authentication.
- **Relevant Patents:** 8, 5, 2
- **Probability of Fit:** MEDIUM-HIGH

## 2. NAVY SBIR OPPORTUNITIES

### 2.1 NAVSEA -- Submarine Stealth and Performance

**Recurring Topic Areas:**
- Advanced submarine hull treatments and drag reduction
- Acoustic signature reduction for undersea platforms
- Submarine auxiliary systems and survivability

**Proposal Concept A:** "Scalar Refractive Field Hydrodynamic Drag Reduction for Submarine Applications"

- **Phase I:** Scale-model demonstration of psi-field bubble formation in water. Measure drag reduction at submarine-relevant Reynolds numbers. Design conformal panel integration for submarine hull.
- **Phase II:** Large-scale water tunnel testing. Closed-loop MPC demonstration. Acoustic signature measurement in anechoic tank.
- **Relevant Patents:** 1, 2
- **Budget:** Phase I ~$250K, Phase II ~$1M
- **POC Alignment:** NAVSEA 05T (Submarine Technology), NUWC Newport

**Proposal Concept B:** "Conformal Acoustic Cloaking via Controlled Scalar Refractive Envelopes"

- **Phase I:** Theoretical analysis of acoustic cloaking effectiveness vs. psi-field intensity and geometry. Design cloaking panel array for representative submarine cross-section.
- **Phase II:** Anechoic chamber testing of psi-field acoustic cloaking at relevant frequencies.
- **Relevant Patents:** 1, 2
- **POC Alignment:** NAVSEA 05U, PMS 450 (Virginia-class submarine program)

### 2.2 NAVAIR / ONR -- Hypersonic Flight

**Recurring Topic Areas:**
- Hypersonic vehicle thermal management
- Hypersonic guidance and control
- Advanced aerodynamic concepts

**Proposal Concept:** "Scalar Refractive Corridor for Hypersonic Drag and Thermal Load Reduction"

- **Phase I:** CFD modeling of psi-corridor formation at Mach 5-10. Predict drag reduction and thermal load reduction. Design wind-tunnel test article.
- **Phase II:** Hypersonic wind tunnel demonstration of psi-corridor drag reduction.
- **Relevant Patents:** 1, 2, 9
- **POC Alignment:** ONR Code 351 (Aerospace Science), NAVAIR 4.0

### 2.3 SPAWAR/NAVWAR -- Undersea Communications

**Recurring Topic Areas:**
- Underwater wireless communications
- Submarine communications at depth
- Autonomous undersea vehicle communications

**Proposal Concept:** "Scalar Refractive Field Undersea Communication Link"

- **Phase I:** Theoretical analysis of psi-corridor propagation in seawater. Design transmitter/receiver pair for underwater operation. Model data rate vs. range.
- **Phase II:** Pool/tank demonstration of psi-communication link in water. Measure data rate, range, and error rate.
- **Relevant Patents:** 8, 2
- **POC Alignment:** NAVWAR PEO C4I, NUWC Division Newport undersea warfare

### 2.4 Naval Research Laboratory (NRL)

**Recurring Topic Areas:** NRL SBIR topics span basic and applied research across Navy-relevant sciences.

**Proposal Concept:** "Fundamental Characterization of Electromagnetically Driven Scalar Refractive Fields in Dense Media"

- **Phase I:** Laboratory construction and characterization of psi-resonator. Measure psi-field properties in water and air. Compare measurements to DFD theoretical predictions.
- **Phase II:** Scale-up characterization. Multi-panel array. Acoustic and hydrodynamic interaction studies.
- **Relevant Patents:** 2, 1
- **POC Alignment:** NRL Code 5500 (Acoustics), Code 6100 (Optical Sciences)

## 3. ARMY SBIR OPPORTUNITIES

### 3.1 PEO IEW&S -- Position, Navigation, and Timing

**Recurring Topic Areas:**
- GNSS-denied navigation for ground forces
- Anti-jamming and anti-spoofing PNT
- Dismounted soldier navigation
- Urban and underground navigation

**Proposal Concept:** "Scalar Refractive Field Navigation System for GNSS-Denied Ground Operations"

- **Phase I:** Design tactical psi-PNT emitter and receiver. Model accuracy in urban/underground environments. Develop rapid deployment concept for forward operating bases.
- **Phase II:** Prototype tactical emitter/receiver pair. Field demonstration in GPS-denied environment (building interior, tunnel).
- **Relevant Patents:** 9, 5
- **Budget:** Phase I ~$250K, Phase II ~$1.1M
- **POC Alignment:** PEO IEW&S PM PNT, CCDC C5ISR PNT Division

### 3.2 CCDC Army Research Lab (ARL) -- Electronic Warfare

**Recurring Topic Areas:**
- Novel communication modalities for EW-contested environments
- Cognitive electronic warfare
- Spectrum management and alternative bands

**Proposal Concept:** "Non-Electromagnetic Communication Channel via Scalar Refractive Field for EW-Immune Operations"

- **Phase I:** Theoretical analysis of psi-channel properties from an EW perspective. Model adversary detection probability. Design man-portable psi-communication terminal.
- **Phase II:** Prototype tactical psi-communication node. Test in EW-contested environment (with friendly jamming to demonstrate immunity).
- **Relevant Patents:** 8, 2
- **POC Alignment:** ARL SEDD (Sensors and Electron Devices), CCDC C5ISR EW Division

### 3.3 CCDC ARL -- Quantum Information Science

**Recurring Topic Areas:**
- Quantum computing hardware and software for Army applications
- Quantum sensing for PNT and ISR
- Quantum networking

**Proposal Concept:** "Scalar Refractive Field Stabilization of Quantum Sensors for Army PNT and ISR"

- **Phase I:** Design psi-field stabilization architecture for atom interferometer-based inertial sensors. Model decoherence suppression in field-deployable quantum sensors.
- **Phase II:** Integrate psi-stabilization with commercial atom interferometer. Measure performance improvement in uncontrolled (non-laboratory) environment.
- **Relevant Patents:** 3, 6, 9
- **POC Alignment:** ARL Quantum Science Division

## 4. AIR FORCE / SPACE FORCE SBIR OPPORTUNITIES

### 4.1 AFRL -- Hypersonic Vehicles

**Recurring Topic Areas:**
- Thermal protection systems for sustained hypersonic flight
- Hypersonic vehicle drag reduction
- Air-breathing hypersonic propulsion

**Proposal Concept:** "Scalar Refractive Corridor for Sustained Hypersonic Cruise: Thermal and Drag Management"

- **Phase I:** High-fidelity CFD modeling of psi-corridor interaction with hypersonic flow. Predict thermal load and drag reduction. Design arc-jet test article.
- **Phase II:** Arc-jet and wind tunnel testing of psi-corridor effect on thermal loads and drag at Mach 5-8.
- **Relevant Patents:** 1, 2
- **POC Alignment:** AFRL Aerospace Systems Directorate (AFRL/RQ)

### 4.2 Space Force -- Resilient PNT for Space Operations

**Recurring Topic Areas:**
- Alternative PNT for cislunar operations
- Space domain awareness sensor technology
- Satellite protection and resilience

**Proposal Concept A:** "Scalar Refractive Field PNT for Cislunar Space Operations"

- **Phase I:** Model psi-PNT architecture for cislunar space. Design emitter constellation for Moon-Earth-Lagrange point coverage. Analyze position and timing accuracy.
- **Phase II:** Ground-based emitter/receiver demonstration. Performance validation at increasing ranges.
- **Relevant Patents:** 9, 5
- **POC Alignment:** Space Systems Command (SSC), SpaceWERX

**Proposal Concept B:** "Quantum-Linked Scalar Field Sensor Array for Space Domain Awareness"

- **Phase I:** Design psi-linked sensor array architecture for space-based deployment. Model detection sensitivity and angular resolution for cislunar object tracking.
- **Phase II:** Prototype ground-based psi-linked sensor pair. Demonstrate phase correlation and joint detection performance.
- **Relevant Patents:** 6, 2
- **POC Alignment:** Space Systems Command S9, Space RCO

### 4.3 AFRL -- Directed Energy Defense

**Recurring Topic Areas:**
- Countermeasures against directed energy weapons
- Platform protection technologies
- Adaptive defense systems

**Proposal Concept:** "Active Refractive Shield for Protection Against Directed Energy Weapons"

- **Phase I:** Theoretical analysis of psi-field beam deflection. Design laboratory-scale deflection experiment. Model shield performance vs. threat power and wavelength.
- **Phase II:** Laboratory demonstration of laser beam deflection through engineered psi-gradient. Measure deflection angle, residual fluence, and shield bandwidth.
- **Relevant Patents:** 1, 2, 7
- **POC Alignment:** AFRL Directed Energy Directorate (AFRL/RD), Kirtland AFB

## 7. PHASE I PROPOSAL TEMPLATE ELEMENTS

For each SBIR Phase I proposal, the following structure is recommended:

### Technical Volume (25 pages max, typically)

1. **Identification and Significance of the Problem**
   - Reference specific DoD capability gap
   - Cite relevant DoD strategic documents (NDS, service-specific strategies)
   - Quantify the cost/impact of the gap

2. **Technical Objectives**
   - State 3-4 specific, measurable objectives for Phase I
   - Example: "Demonstrate psi-field amplitude of X at Y distance from resonator"

3. **Technical Approach**
   - Describe DFD theoretical foundation (brief, accessible)
   - Detail the specific hardware to be built and experiments to be performed
   - Reference patent claims that will be validated
   - Provide calculations showing feasibility

4. **Phase I Deliverables**
   - Hardware demonstration results
   - Measurement data compared to theoretical predictions
   - Phase II plan with detailed milestones

5. **Commercialization Strategy**
   - Dual-use applications (commercial shipping, aviation, telecommunications)
   - Path to Phase III (DoD acquisition) via specific program of record
   - Market size for commercial applications

6. **Key Personnel**
   - Inventor (Gary Thomas Alcock) as PI
   - Additional team members: RF engineer, experimental physicist, metamaterial designer
   - Advisory board from relevant defense community

7. **Facilities and Equipment**
   - Laboratory space for resonator construction and testing
   - Access to precision measurement equipment
   - Water tunnel access (for drag reduction proposals)

## 8. KEY CONTACTS AND ENGAGEMENT STRATEGY

### Pre-Proposal Engagement (Critical for Success)

**DARPA Program Managers:** Request pre-proposal meetings with DSO, TTO, MTO, and STO PMs. Present 2-page concept summaries. Ask about upcoming Broad Agency Announcements (BAAs) that might fit.

**Navy Technical Points of Contact:**
- NUWC Newport: Submarine stealth and undersea systems
- NRL: Basic research in acoustics and optical sciences
- NAVSEA 05T: Submarine technology requirements

**Army Technical Points of Contact:**
- CCDC C5ISR: PNT and EW requirements
- ARL: Quantum science and basic research

**Air Force/Space Force:**
- AFRL/RQ: Hypersonic vehicle technology
- AFRL/RD: Directed energy
- SpaceWERX: Space innovation

### Recommended Sequence

1. **Month 1-2:** Prepare 2-page white papers for each proposal concept
2. **Month 2-3:** Request pre-proposal meetings with target PMs
3. **Month 3-4:** Incorporate PM feedback; refine proposals
4. **Month 4-5:** Submit Phase I proposals to open solicitations
5. **Month 6-12:** Execute Phase I awards; prepare Phase II proposals
6. **Month 12-18:** Pursue OTA opportunities with DIU for most mature technologies
