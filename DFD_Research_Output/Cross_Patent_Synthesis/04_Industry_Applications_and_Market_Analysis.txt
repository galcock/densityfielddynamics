================================================================================

The DFD patent portfolio creates MULTIPLE layers of competitive advantage:

1. UNIFIED PHYSICS: All patents derive from the same physical principle
   (psi scalar refractive field). A competitor must either license the
   entire framework or develop an alternative physics, which is not known
   to exist.

2. HARDWARE COMMONALITY: The same metamaterial panels, resonators, and
   control systems serve multiple markets. Manufacturing scale for
   defense applications (high-value, early adoption) drives cost reduction
   for civilian applications (high-volume, later adoption).

3. NETWORK EFFECTS: Psi infrastructure (Atlas design) becomes more
   valuable with each node added. Power + comms + navigation in one
   system creates switching costs that prevent competitor entry.

4. SELF-REINFORCING ECOSYSTEM: P2 resonators enable all other patents.
   P7 energy harvesting powers all other patents. P10 computing controls
   all other patents. The portfolio is a self-reinforcing technology
   ecosystem, not a collection of independent inventions.

5. DEEP IP MOAT: 15 existing patents + 7 proposed new patents = 22
   patents covering the foundational physics, all 10 major hardware
   categories, and 7 application domains. Combined with 30+ published
   theoretical papers, this represents the deepest IP position in
   scalar refractive field technology worldwide.
