\item Require understanding the spectral measure of the full (non-truncated) $\CP^2$ Dirac operator in the spectral action framework.
\item This is a deep mathematical problem related to noncommutative geometry and spectral triples.
\end{enumerate}
Estimated difficulty: Very high. Timeline: $> 2028$.

\subsection{DFD at Finite Temperature}
v3.3 treats DFD at $T = 0$ (vacuum). Finite-temperature extension would:
\begin{enumerate}[nosep]
\item Describe the DFD early universe (reheating, baryogenesis).
\item Predict cosmological phase transition signals (gravitational wave background from phase transitions).
\item Compute thermal corrections to $\alpha$ (running with temperature).
\end{enumerate}
Estimated difficulty: High. Timeline: $> 2027$.

\subsection{DFD on Curved Backgrounds}

\end{enumerate}

\section{NF75-6: Honest Assessment of Risk}
%=============================================================================

\subsection{What Could Go Wrong}
\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Euclid DR1 finds $E_G$ consistent with GR to $< 1\%$}: DFD $E_G$ prediction (2--4\%) would be disfavoured. BUT: DFD predicts $\epsilon(z)$ rises with $z$; if lowest-$z$ bins show no signal, high-$z$ bins may still be consistent.
\item \textbf{DESI Y3 finds $w_a = 0.00 \pm 0.03$}: DFD's $w_a = -0.12$ at $4\sigma$ tension. Serious problem. Would require reassessing $\rho$-field late-time dynamics.
\item \textbf{CMB-S4 finds $r < 0.002$ at $95\%$ CL}: DFD's $r = 0.004$ excluded at $> 2\sigma$. Would require reassessing inflationary sector.
\item \textbf{JUNO finds inverted hierarchy}: DFD assumes normal hierarchy. Inverted hierarchy would invalidate $\Sigma m_\nu = 61.4$ meV.
\item \textbf{New $\alpha$ measurement agrees better with another theory}: Currently no competitor at sub-ppm. But if one emerges, DFD's 0.55 ppm residual becomes a weakness.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{What Must Go Right}
\begin{enumerate}
\item At least ONE of: $E_G$ detection, $r$ detection, $w_a < 0$ detection.
\item No measurement in $> 3\sigma$ tension with DFD.
\item Normal neutrino mass hierarchy confirmed.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Overall Risk Assessment}
\textbf{MODERATE.} DFD makes many specific, falsifiable predictions. The probability of ALL predictions being confirmed is not high. But the probability of at least some being confirmed (given current data trends) is substantial. The theory's intellectual honesty (honest negatives, classified YELLOW items, error budgets) positions it well regardless of outcome.
