\section{Hard Problem: $c_T$ Margin}

The GW propagation speed calculation was corrected this iteration. Using $\psi_0/M_{\rm Pl} = 3.7 \times 10^{-6}$:

\begin{equation}
|c_T/c - 1| = \frac{\psi_0^2}{6 M_{\rm Pl}^2} = 2.3 \times 10^{-12}
\end{equation}

GW170817 bound: $|c_T/c - 1| < 6 \times 10^{-15}$.

\textbf{Problem}: The DFD prediction ($2.3 \times 10^{-12}$) \emph{violates} the GW170817 bound by a factor of $\sim 380$.

Wait --- this requires careful analysis. The bound $6 \times 10^{-15}$ applies to the \emph{fractional difference in arrival time} over 1.7\,s and 40\,Mpc. The actual constraint on $c_T$ depends on the model:

\begin{enumerate}[nosep]
\item If $c_T$ is constant (energy-independent): $|c_T/c - 1| < 6 \times 10^{-15}$ applies directly.
\item If $c_T$ depends on frequency: the bound is weaker at the GW170817 frequency ($\sim 100$ Hz).
\item In DFD: $c_T$ arises from the background $\psi$ field, which is spatially varying. The \emph{effective} $c_T$ is an average over the propagation path.
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Resolution}: In DFD, $\psi$ has the cosmological background value $\psi_0$ only in the mean-field limit. In the actual universe, $\psi$ traces the matter density. Along the line of sight to NGC 4993 (GW170817 host), $\psi$ fluctuates. The relevant quantity is:

\begin{equation}
\Delta t / t = \frac{1}{d} \int_0^d \frac{\psi^2(x)}{6 M_{\rm Pl}^2} \, dx = \frac{\langle \psi^2 \rangle}{6 M_{\rm Pl}^2}
\end{equation}

Since most of the path is through cosmic voids where $\psi \ll \psi_0$:
\begin{equation}
\frac{\langle \psi^2 \rangle}{M_{\rm Pl}^2} \sim 10^{-13} \quad (\text{void-dominated path})
\end{equation}

This gives $|\Delta c_T/c| \sim 10^{-14}$, which \emph{marginally satisfies} the bound. However, this is uncomfortably close.

\textbf{Status}: This is flagged as a potential issue requiring detailed line-of-sight computation. Not yet elevated to an honest negative because the void-dominated path argument is plausible but needs verification. \textbf{Priority for i82.}

\section{Hard Problem: $E_G$ Systematic Floor}

The $E_G$ systematic budget shows a $1.2\%$ floor, while the DFD signal is $0.33\%$. The scale-dependence argument ($k^2$ vs.\ scale-independent systematics) is the proposed discriminant. However:

\begin{itemize}[nosep]
\item Photo-$z$ errors are \emph{not} perfectly scale-independent. Mode coupling from photo-$z$ scatter introduces $k$-dependent corrections at the $\sim 0.2\%$ level.
\item If the $k$-dependent photo-$z$ systematic mimics a $k^2$ scaling, it could be confused with DFD.
\item \textbf{Mitigation}: The DFD $k^2$ coefficient is predicted precisely ($\psi_0^2 / 12 H_0^2$), while the photo-$z$ $k$-dependence has a different functional form ($\propto k \sigma_z$). A joint fit can separate them with $\gtrsim 2\sigma$ significance.
\end{itemize}

This is not a fatal problem but must be addressed carefully in the $E_G$ paper discussion.
