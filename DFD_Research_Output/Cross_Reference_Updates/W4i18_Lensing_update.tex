\section{Galactic Lensing: Crisis-Immune}
%=============================================================================

Galactic-scale lensing in DFD derives from:
\begin{equation}
\Phi_{\rm lens} = \Phi_N + \frac{1}{2}\psi c^2 \ln(r/r_0)
\end{equation}
where $\Phi_N$ is the Newtonian potential and the $\psi$ contribution
comes from the \emph{static} field sourced by the galaxy's mass
distribution. This is the Newtonian limit (Sector A), with:

\begin{itemize}
\item $\psi$ sourced by $\nabla^2\psi = -4\pi G\rho\,\mu/\mu_0$.
\item Deflection angle modified by factor $1 + \delta_\psi(r)$.
\item In deep MOND: $n_\psi \to \sqrt{2}$ (effective refractive index).
\item In Newtonian regime: $n_\psi \to 1 + \epsilon$ with $\epsilon$
  small.
\end{itemize}

No cosmological perturbation growth enters this calculation.

  it is a galactic/cluster-scale issue (Sector A), not a cosmological
