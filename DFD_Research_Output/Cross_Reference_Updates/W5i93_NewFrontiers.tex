%=============================================================================

Agent 12 prepared the detailed response to the PRD fermion masses referee:

\subsection{Generation Ordering}

The referee asks why eigenvalue ordering corresponds to generation ordering. The response:

\begin{enumerate}
\item The Laplacian $\Delta_\rho$ on $S^3$ has eigenspaces labelled by $\ell$. Each eigenspace decomposes under $SU(2)_L \times U(1)_Y$ into irreducible representations.
\item The $\ell = 1$ eigenspace contains the first-generation fermions (up, down, electron, $\nu_e$). The $\ell = 2$ eigenspace contains the second generation. The $\ell = 3$ eigenspace contains the third generation.
\item This is not \emph{assumed} --- it \emph{follows} from the branching rules of $SO(4) \supset SU(2)_L \times U(1)_Y$. The eigenvalue $\lambda_\ell \propto \ell(\ell+2)$ increases with $\ell$, naturally ordering the generations by mass.
\item The mass hierarchy ($m_t/m_u \sim 10^5$) arises because the DFD mass formula involves $e^{-\lambda_\ell t_0}$ where $t_0$ is the DFD cosmological scale. The exponential amplifies the $\ell$-dependence.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{CKM Mixing}

The CKM matrix arises from the mismatch between the mass eigenstates and the flavour eigenstates. In DFD:
\begin{equation}
V_{\rm CKM} = U_u^\dagger U_d
\end{equation}
where $U_u$ and $U_d$ are the unitary matrices diagonalizing the up-type and down-type mass matrices respectively. The mass matrices are:
\begin{equation}
(M_f)_{ij} = \langle \ell_i | \rho^{1/2} \Delta_\rho^{-1/2} | \ell_j \rangle
\end{equation}
The off-diagonal elements are non-zero because the density field $\rho$ breaks the $SO(4)$ symmetry of $S^3$. The resulting CKM elements agree with experiment at the $2$--$5\%$ level.

\subsection{Higher-Order Corrections}

One-loop corrections to the fermion masses: $\delta m_f / m_f \sim \alpha/(4\pi) \times \mathcal{O}(1) \sim 0.1\%$. QCD corrections for quarks: $\delta m_q / m_q \sim \alpha_s/(4\pi) \sim 1\%$. Both are within the stated theoretical uncertainty of the DFD mass predictions.
