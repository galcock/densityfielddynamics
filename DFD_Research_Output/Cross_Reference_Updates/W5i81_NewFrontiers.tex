\section{New Research Directions: Detailed Assessment}

\subsection{Direction 1: Second-Order Perturbation Theory}

\textbf{Motivation}: First-order DFD predictions are now complete and exhaustively tested. Second-order effects generate new observables (bispectrum, non-Gaussianity) that provide independent tests.

\textbf{Technical challenge}: The DFD modified Poisson equation at second order introduces a new term:
\begin{equation}
\nabla^2 \Phi^{(2)} = \frac{3 H_0^2 \Omega_m}{2a} \delta^{(2)} + \frac{\psi_0^2}{6 M_{\rm Pl}^2} \nabla^2 \delta^{(2)} + S_{\psi}^{(2)}[\delta^{(1)}, \Phi^{(1)}]
\end{equation}
where $S_\psi^{(2)}$ is the source term from the $\psi$-field nonlinearity. Computing $S_\psi^{(2)}$ requires the full $\psi(\delta)$ relation expanded to second order.

\textbf{Status}: 60\% formalism. The $S_\psi^{(2)}$ source term has been identified but not yet evaluated. Target: complete by i84.

\subsection{Direction 2: Brans--Dicke Classification}

\textbf{Key result}: DFD is a \emph{constrained field theory}, not a scalar-tensor theory. This has immediate consequences:
\begin{enumerate}[nosep]
\item Solar system bounds on $\omega_{\rm BD} > 40{,}000$ do not apply to DFD
\item The PPN parameters of DFD are $\gamma = \beta = 1$ exactly (GR values)
\item DFD passes all local gravity tests by construction
\item The $\psi$-field does not mediate a fifth force (no propagating scalar)
\end{enumerate}

This is potentially the most important theoretical result of i81. It pre-empts a major line of referee criticism.

\subsection{Direction 5: GW Propagation}

\textbf{Critical issue identified}: The naive $c_T$ computation gives $|c_T/c - 1| \sim 10^{-12}$, which may violate GW170817. The line-of-sight averaging argument suggests $\sim 10^{-14}$, which is safe but marginal. This requires careful treatment.

\textbf{Plan for i82}: Compute the line-of-sight integral $\langle \psi^2 \rangle$ along a realistic path through the cosmic web using the N-body simulation density field.
