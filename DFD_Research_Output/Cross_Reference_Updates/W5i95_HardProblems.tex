\subsection{What Could Go Wrong?}

\textbf{Finding 302}: Risk assessment for i96--i100.

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{PRL Rejection (P = 60\%)}: The most likely negative outcome. If PRL rejects, the $\alpha$ paper can be resubmitted to PRD or CQG (where we have a track record). Impact: reputational, not scientific. The result is correct regardless of PRL's decision.

\item \textbf{MNRAS N-body I: Major Revision (P = 50\%)}: N-body papers frequently receive major revision requests. If so, the response would take 1--2 iterations. Impact: delays acceptance but does not change the science.

\item \textbf{Euclid DR1 Delay (P = 20\%)}: If delayed past October 2026, we cannot do the rapid analysis. Impact: Y2 remains YELLOW; aspirational deliverables missed. Not a programme failure.

\item \textbf{Unexpected Observational Conflict (P = 5\%)}: A new dataset contradicts DFD at $> 3\sigma$. Impact: would require a new honest negative and potentially a YELLOW or RED item. Very unlikely given current data consistency.
\end{enumerate}
