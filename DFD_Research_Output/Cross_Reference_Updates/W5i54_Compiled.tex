\item \textbf{CLOCK SIGNAL SCREENED BY EFE (Agent 3, Grade A)}\\

The screening factor $a_0/|\mathbf{a}_N^{\mathrm{local}}| \sim 2 \times 10^{-8}$ reduces the signal to $\sim 7 \times 10^{-21}$, three orders of magnitude below current $10^{-18}$ clock precision. \textit{Impact}: CRITICAL. Removes a headline prediction. Demonstrates the programme's self-correction.

\item \textbf{$S_8$ SCALE-DEPENDENCE DIRECTION ISSUE (Agent 14, Grade A)}\\
DFD's MOND enhancement increases the growth factor at large scales. Under a $\Lambda$CDM analysis, this excess power should be fitted with a HIGHER $\sigma_8$, producing $S_8^{\mathrm{large}} > S_8^{\mathrm{small}}$. The observed $S_8$ tension has $S_8^{\mathrm{large}} < S_8^{\mathrm{small}}$. Either (a) non-linear mode coupling reverses the effect, (b) the DFD modification enters at scales below those probed by WL, or (c) the $S_8$ prediction is wrong.

\textit{Impact}: HIGH. The i53 $S_8$ prediction is DEMOTED from \GREEN\ to \YELLOW\ pending full halo model computation.

\item \textbf{gCAMB PHASE 0A PATHWAY (Agent 16, Grade A)}\\
gCAMB's built-in $\mu(a,k)/\Sigma(a,k)$ parameterisation matches DFD's structure. Implementation requires $\sim$50 lines of CUDA code modification. Timeline: 8 weeks. \textit{Impact}: HIGHEST STRATEGIC. If executed, Phase 0A --- the critical bottleneck since i41 --- would finally be broken.

\item \textbf{$R_{31}$ CONFIRMED AT $0.429 \pm 0.003$ (Agent 1, Grade A)}\\
Two independent analytic calculations (i53 Agent~5 and i54 Agent~1) agree: $R_{31}^{\mathrm{DFD}} = 0.429$--$0.430$, matching Planck's $0.430 \pm 0.010$ at $< 0.1\sigma$. The baryon-loading correction is refined from $+0.003$ (i53) to $+0.002$ (i54). Tentative \YELLOW\ $\to$ \GREEN\ upgrade for CMB $R_{31}$ stands.

\textit{Impact}: HIGH. Solidifies the i53 result with independent confirmation.

\item \textbf{$a_6$ COMPUTED --- WRONG SIGN AND TOO LARGE (Agent 7, Grade B+)}\\
The explicit gauge-sector $a_6$ on $\CP^2$ gives $\delta\alpha^{-1}/\alpha^{-1} \approx -1.1 \times 10^{-6}$ (negative, at order $k_{\max}^{-2}$). The observed residual is $+5.6 \times 10^{-9}$ (positive). The $a_6$ correction is $200\times$ too large with the wrong sign. The $\alpha$ residual does NOT come from $a_6$ alone. Possible $\CP^2$--$S^3$ cancellation or $a_8$ origin.

\textit{Impact}: HIGH. Closes the $a_6$ avenue (at least in its simple form). Opens $a_8$ and cancellation mechanisms.

\item \textbf{K\"AHLER VANISHING: CUBIC GAUGE $a_6$ IS ZERO (Agent 2, Grade A)}\\
On $\CP^2$ with the Fubini--Study metric, $F_{\mu\nu}F^{\nu\alpha}F_{\alpha}^{\ \mu} = 0$ because $J^2 = -\mathrm{id}$ (complex structure). The cubic gauge term in $a_6$ vanishes identically. The surviving gauge $a_6$ is the Riemann--gauge coupling at $k_{\max}^{-2}$, not $k_{\max}^{-3}$ as i53 estimated. This is a rigorous differential geometry result.

\textit{Impact}: MEDIUM-HIGH. Corrects i53 and reveals a beautiful geometric structure.

\item \textbf{$\Sigma m_\nu$ BOUND CORRECTED TO 77 meV (Agent 11, Grade A)}\\
The self-consistent DFD-modified cosmological bound is $77$ meV (95\% CL), not $90 \pm 15$ meV (i52). The main correction: the distance-bias relaxation is smaller ($\sim 2$ meV, not $\sim 10$ meV) because CMB tightly constrains $\Omega_m h^2$. DFD's $61.4$ meV prediction has $16$ meV margin ($\sim 1.1\sigma$).

\textit{Impact}: MEDIUM. Tightens the assessment. DFD is safe but not comfortable.

\item \textbf{EUCLID PRE-REGISTRATION: 5 PREDICTIONS (Agent 13, Grade A)}\\
Five specific, quantitative predictions for Euclid October 2026: (E1) $S_8(R)$ transition with $R_\times \approx 8\,h^{-1}$ Mpc (DEMOTED to \YELLOW); (E2) BAO peak position unchanged to $< 0.1\%$; (E3) lensing/dynamics mass ratio $\eta_\psi = 0.02 \pm 0.01$; (E4) galaxy--galaxy lensing excess at $r > 100$ kpc; (E5) scale-dependent intrinsic alignment signal. E2--E4 remain valid.

\textit{Impact}: HIGH STRATEGIC. Publishing these before October would demonstrate DFD's predictive power.

\item \textbf{CHERN--SIMONS BIREFRINGENCE VIABLE (Agent 9, Grade B+)}\\
A Chern--Simons coupling $g_{\mathrm{CS}} \approx 0.05$ reproduces $\beta = 0.30^\circ$ from the cosmological $\Delta\psi \approx 0.11$. Local constraints are satisfied. The $\CP^2$ second Chern class gives $g_{\mathrm{CS}} \approx 0.031$, close to required value. If derived from spectral geometry, this would be parameter-free. Costs one new parameter if phenomenological.

\textit{Impact}: MEDIUM. Opens a pathway to accommodate birefringence without breaking the core theory.

\item \textbf{$KO$-DIMENSION MISMATCH (Agent 10, Grade B)}\\
The product geometry $M^4 \times \CP^2 \times S^3 \times F$ has $d_{KO} = 17 \equiv 1 \pmod{8}$, not the standard $0 \pmod{8}$. This is a known subtlety in NCG models with extra dimensions, not a fatal error, but requires mathematical resolution. The Spin$^c$ elimination claim (i52) is likely correct locally on $\CP^2$ but needs the global proof completed.

\textit{Impact}: MEDIUM. Mathematical issue, not physics-breaking. Needs resolution for v3.3.
\end{enumerate}
