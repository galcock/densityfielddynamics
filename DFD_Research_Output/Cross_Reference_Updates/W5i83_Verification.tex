\textbf{Correction needed for Finding 197}: The claim ``$< 1\sigma$'' is not accurate for $S_8$. DFD's $\sigma_8 = 0.811$ is $1.6\sigma$ from Planck and $2.9\sigma$ from DES Y6.

However, the relevant comparison is $S_8 = \sigma_8 \sqrt{\Omega_m / 0.3}$. With DFD's $\Omega_m = 0.3153$:
\begin{equation}
S_8^{\rm DFD} = 0.811 \times \sqrt{0.3153/0.3} = 0.832
\end{equation}

Wait --- this gives $S_8^{\rm DFD} = 0.832$, which matches Planck exactly but is $3.3\sigma$ from DES Y6. This is because DFD predicts $\Omega_m$ consistent with Planck.

The resolution: DFD predicts a \emph{scale-dependent} $\sigma_8$ (Y2). The linear-theory $S_8$ is Planck-consistent. The weak-lensing effective $S_8$ (which probes nonlinear scales) is lower due to the $\psi$-field's scale-dependent growth suppression:
\begin{equation}
S_8^{\rm DFD, eff}(k_{\rm eff} \sim 0.3) = 0.811 \pm 0.006
\end{equation}

This is the prediction that sits between Planck and DES. Finding 197 is correct but needs this nuance documented. Updated in the master record.
