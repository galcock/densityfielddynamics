\section{Critical Verification: $c_T = c$ Exactly}

This is the most important verification of i82. The claim is that the $\psi$-field does not modify gravitational wave propagation speed.

\subsection{Three Independent Verifications}

\textbf{Method 1: Action Expansion (Agent 8).}

Start from the DFD action:
\begin{equation}
S_{\rm DFD} = \int d^4x \sqrt{-g} \left[\frac{M_{\rm Pl}^2}{2} R + \mathcal{L}_m + \mathcal{L}_\psi(\psi, T^\mu{}_\mu)\right]
\end{equation}

The tensor perturbation $h_{ij}$ satisfies $g^{\mu\nu} h_{\mu\nu} = 0$ (traceless). Since $\mathcal{L}_\psi$ depends on $T^\mu{}_\mu$ and the trace of $h_{ij}$ vanishes, the variation $\delta \mathcal{L}_\psi / \delta h_{ij} = 0$ at linear order in $h_{ij}$.

Therefore, the tensor equation of motion is:
\begin{equation}
\ddot{h}_{ij} + 3H\dot{h}_{ij} - \frac{\nabla^2}{a^2} h_{ij} = 16\pi G \, \Pi_{ij}^{TT}
\end{equation}
which is \emph{identical} to GR. The anisotropic stress $\Pi_{ij}^{TT}$ from standard matter is unmodified by $\psi$.

\textbf{Method 2: Effective Field Theory of Dark Energy (Agent 9).}

In the EFTofDE framework (Gubitosi et al.\ 2013), the GW speed is:
\begin{equation}
c_T^2 = 1 + \alpha_T, \quad \alpha_T = -\frac{2 \dot{f}}{f} \quad \text{(for } S \supset f(t) \, {}^{(3)}R \text{)}
\end{equation}

In DFD, the $\psi$-field does not multiply the 3-Ricci scalar. The spatial curvature coupling is standard ($f = M_{\rm Pl}^2/2 = \text{const}$). Therefore $\dot{f} = 0$ and $\alpha_T = 0$ exactly.

\textbf{Method 3: Characteristic Analysis (Agent 7).}

The characteristic speeds of the DFD system were computed by extracting the principal symbol of the field equations. The system has:
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
\item 2 tensor modes propagating at $c$ (GW polarisations)
\item 1 scalar mode with zero propagation speed ($\psi$ is algebraically constrained)
\item 2 vector modes (pure gauge, as in GR)
\end{itemize}

Total propagating degrees of freedom: 2. Identical to GR.

\section{BD Letter Verification}

Agent 17's proof that no $\omega_{\rm BD}$ mapping exists was verified:

\textbf{Claim}: There is no value of $\omega_{\rm BD}$ for which the BD field equations reduce to the DFD equations.

\textbf{Proof sketch}:
\begin{enumerate}[nosep]
\item BD: $\Box \phi = T / (3 + 2\omega_{\rm BD})$, with $\phi$ dynamical.
\item DFD: $\psi^2 = \psi_0^2 h(\delta_m)$, algebraic constraint.
\item Set $\phi = \psi^2$. Then $\Box \psi^2 = 2\psi \Box \psi + 2 (\partial \psi)^2$.
\item For $\Box \psi^2 = T/(3 + 2\omega_{\rm BD})$, need $\psi$ to be dynamical.
\item But $\psi$ is algebraically constrained: $\partial_\mu \psi = (\psi_0^2 / 2\delta_c \psi) \partial_\mu \delta_m$.
\item Therefore $\Box \psi^2 = \psi_0^2 \Box(\delta_m / \delta_c) + \ldots$, which depends on the matter dynamics.
\item This cannot be matched to $T/(3 + 2\omega_{\rm BD})$ for any constant $\omega_{\rm BD}$ because the left-hand side depends on $\Box \delta_m$, while $T$ depends on $\delta_m$ (without derivatives in the non-relativistic limit).
\end{enumerate}

The proof is valid. DFD $\neq$ BD for any $\omega_{\rm BD}$.

\section{Finding 192 Verification: Propagating Degrees of Freedom}

\begin{enumerate}[nosep]
