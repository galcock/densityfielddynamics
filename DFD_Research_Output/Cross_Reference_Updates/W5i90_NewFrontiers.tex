\section{Key Research Questions for the Next Decade}

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Is DFD correct?} Euclid + DESI + CMB-S4 will answer this at $> 5\sigma$ by $\sim 2030$. The pre-registered predictions provide a clean, falsifiable test.

\item \textbf{Can the $\alpha$ derivation be independently verified?} The lattice Monte Carlo approach is described in the supplemental material. An independent group reproducing $\alpha^{-1} = 137.036$ from the spectral action would be definitive.

\item \textbf{What is the UV completion of DFD?} The current framework is effective at scales above the Planck length. Connecting DFD to a full quantum gravity theory is the deepest open question.

\item \textbf{Does DFD explain dark matter?} DFD's MOND-like behaviour in the deep-MOND regime reproduces galaxy rotation curves without dark matter. But the cosmological success ($S_8$, BAO, CMB) requires further investigation to ensure the baryon-only model is fully consistent.

\item \textbf{Can DFD be falsified?} Yes: (i) a multiply-lensed GW event, (ii) $D_L^{\rm EM}/D_L^{\rm GW} = 1.00$ at $z > 0.5$, (iii) $E_G$ flat in $k$ at Euclid precision, (iv) $r < 0.001$ from CMB-S4, (v) $\Sigma m_\nu > 100$ meV. Any one of these falsifies DFD.
\end{enumerate}
