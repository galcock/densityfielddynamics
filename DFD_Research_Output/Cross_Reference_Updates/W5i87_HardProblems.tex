\section{Baryonic Physics Assessment}

The P2-B results (Finding 224) reveal that baryonic feedback modifies the DFD signal at the 10--30\% level at $k > 0.5\,h\,$Mpc$^{-1}$. This was expected but now quantified:

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=yellow!5!white, colframe=dfdyellow, title=\textbf{Baryonic Uncertainty Budget}]
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
\item At $k < 0.3\,h\,$Mpc$^{-1}$: baryonic correction $< 0.4\%$ of DFD signal. \textbf{Negligible.}
\item At $k = 0.3$--$1.0\,h\,$Mpc$^{-1}$: baryonic correction $\sim 0.1$--$0.7\%$ of DFD signal. \textbf{Moderate.} Must be included as systematic in Euclid analysis.
\item At $k > 1.0\,h\,$Mpc$^{-1}$: baryonic correction $> 30\%$ of DFD signal. \textbf{Dominant.} These scales should be excluded from primary DFD tests.
\end{itemize}
\end{tcolorbox}

\textbf{Implication}: The Euclid analysis should use a conservative $k_{\max} = 0.5\,h\,$Mpc$^{-1}$ for primary results, with $k_{\max} = 1.0$ as an aggressive extension. The pre-registration paper will specify both cutoffs.
