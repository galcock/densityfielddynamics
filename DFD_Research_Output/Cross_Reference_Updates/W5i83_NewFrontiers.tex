\section{Strategic Assessment: The 2026 Opportunity Window}

The literature survey reveals a unique opportunity window for DFD:

\begin{enumerate}[nosep]
\item \textbf{$\Lambda$CDM is not broken} --- but $S_8$ tension persists at $3$--$3.5\sigma$. Modified gravity models that address $S_8$ are in demand.

\item \textbf{GW170817 has eliminated most alternatives}. $f(R)$ with generic parameters, covariant Galileon, massive gravity --- all require $c_T \neq c$ and are constrained. DFD's $c_T = c$ is a major advantage.

\item \textbf{Three surveys launching in 2026}: Euclid DR1, SPHEREx, Rubin. All will provide data that DFD can be tested against.

\item \textbf{The field is hungry for testable alternatives}. The ``modified gravity'' community has pivoted from model-building to model-testing. DFD arrives at exactly the right moment.

\item \textbf{Pre-registration is now valued}. The reproducibility movement means pre-registered predictions carry extra weight. DFD's pre-registered predictions (if published before Euclid DR1) would be highly credible.
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Recommendation}: Prioritise the Euclid pre-registration paper. Submit before October 2026. This is the single highest-impact strategic action the programme can take.
