\section{The discrepancy: precise statement}
\label{sec:statement}
%=============================================================================

The quasi-static scalar action (section02\_formalism Eq.~(3)) is:
\begin{equation}
S_\psi = \int dt\,d^3x\left\{
  \frac{a_*^2}{8\pi G}\,W\!\left(\frac{|\nabla\psi|^2}{a_*^2}\right)
  - \frac{c^2}{2}\,\psi(\rho - \bar\rho)
\right\},
\label{eq:action-paper}
\end{equation}
where $a_* = 2a_0/c^2$ has dimensions m$^{-1}$, and $W(y)$ is
dimensionless with $W(0) = 0$, $W'(0) = 1$.

The stated field equation (Eq.~(8)) is:
\begin{equation}
\nabla\cdot\!\left[\mu\!\left(\frac{|\nabla\psi|}{a_*}\right)\nabla\psi\right]
= -\frac{8\pi G}{c^2}\,(\rho - \bar\rho).
\label{eq:FE-paper}
\end{equation}

\textbf{Claim (W4i16):} Varying \eqref{eq:action-paper} does NOT
produce \eqref{eq:FE-paper}. There is a factor of $c^4/4$.

\section{Rigorous variation of the action}
\label{sec:variation}
%=============================================================================

\subsection{Step 1: Functional derivative of the kinetic term}

Define $X \equiv |\nabla\psi|^2/a_*^2$ (dimensionless). The kinetic
Lagrangian density is $\mathcal{L}_K = [a_*^2/(8\pi G)]\,W(X)$.

Under $\psi \to \psi + \delta\psi$:
\begin{align}
\delta X &= \frac{2\,\nabla\psi\cdot\nabla(\delta\psi)}{a_*^2}.
\end{align}
Therefore:
\begin{align}
\delta\mathcal{L}_K &= \frac{a_*^2}{8\pi G}\,W'(X)\,\delta X
= \frac{a_*^2}{8\pi G}\,W'(X)\,\frac{2\,\nabla\psi\cdot\nabla(\delta\psi)}{a_*^2}
\nonumber\\
&= \frac{1}{4\pi G}\,W'(X)\,\nabla\psi\cdot\nabla(\delta\psi).
\label{eq:dLK}
\end{align}

Note: the $a_*^2$ in the prefactor cancels the $a_*^2$ in the
denominator of $\delta X$. This cancellation is exact and is correctly
noted in the paper's ``derivation sketch.''

Integrating by parts (discarding boundary terms):
\begin{equation}
\int d^3x\;\delta\mathcal{L}_K
= -\int d^3x\;\frac{1}{4\pi G}\,\nabla\cdot\!\left[W'(X)\,\nabla\psi\right]\delta\psi.
\label{eq:var-kinetic}
\end{equation}

\subsection{Step 2: Functional derivative of the matter coupling}

The matter Lagrangian density is $\mathcal{L}_M = -(c^2/2)\,\psi\,(\rho - \bar\rho)$.
\begin{equation}
\frac{\delta\mathcal{L}_M}{\delta\psi} = -\frac{c^2}{2}\,(\rho - \bar\rho).
\label{eq:var-matter}
\end{equation}

\subsection{Step 3: Euler--Lagrange equation}

Setting $\delta S_\psi/\delta\psi = 0$:
\begin{equation}
-\frac{1}{4\pi G}\,\nabla\cdot\!\left[W'(X)\,\nabla\psi\right]
- \frac{c^2}{2}\,(\rho - \bar\rho) = 0.
\end{equation}

Rearranging:
\begin{equation}
\boxed{
\nabla\cdot\!\left[W'(X)\,\nabla\psi\right]
= -2\pi G\,c^2\,(\rho - \bar\rho).
}
\label{eq:FE-from-action}
\end{equation}

\subsection{Step 4: Identifying $\mu$}

For the ``simple'' case, $\mu(x) = W'(x^2)$ where $x = |\nabla\psi|/a_*$.
(More generally $\mu(x) = W'(x^2) + 2x^2 W''(x^2)$, but this overall
structure does not affect the prefactor issue.) So \eqref{eq:FE-from-action}
becomes:
\begin{equation}
\nabla\cdot\!\left[\mu\!\left(\frac{|\nabla\psi|}{a_*}\right)\nabla\psi\right]
= -2\pi G\,c^2\,(\rho - \bar\rho).
\label{eq:FE-derived}
\end{equation}

\subsection{Step 5: Comparison with the stated field equation}

The paper states (Eq.~(8)):
\begin{equation}
\nabla\cdot[\mu\,\nabla\psi] = -\frac{8\pi G}{c^2}\,(\rho - \bar\rho).
\tag{\ref{eq:FE-paper}}
\end{equation}

\textbf{Ratio of RHS:}
\begin{equation}
\frac{-2\pi G\,c^2}{-8\pi G/c^2} = \frac{c^4}{4}.
\end{equation}

\textbf{Confirmed:} The action \eqref{eq:action-paper} produces a field
equation that differs from the stated Eq.~(8) by exactly $c^4/4$.

\section{Which equation is physically correct?}
\label{sec:physics}
%=============================================================================

The Newtonian limit requires:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $\mu \to 1$ (high gradients)
\item $\Phi = -c^2\psi/2$ (from the matter coupling / WEP derivation, Eq.~(15)--(16))
\item Poisson equation: $\nabla^2\Phi = 4\pi G\rho$
\end{enumerate}

From (2): $\nabla^2\Phi = -(c^2/2)\nabla^2\psi$.

From (3): $-(c^2/2)\nabla^2\psi = 4\pi G\rho$, hence
$\nabla^2\psi = -8\pi G\rho/c^2$.

This matches Eq.~(8) exactly. It does NOT match the equation derived
from the action, which gives $\nabla^2\psi = -2\pi G c^2\rho$.

\textbf{Conclusion}: The stated field equation Eq.~(8) is physically correct.
The action Eq.~(3)/(6) has the wrong prefactor.

\section{What is the correct action?}
\label{sec:correct-action}
%=============================================================================

We need the variation to produce $-8\pi G\rho/c^2$ on the RHS. Working
backward from the Euler--Lagrange equation:

For the kinetic term to produce $\nabla\cdot[\mu\nabla\psi]$ on the LHS
(as it already does), we need the matter coupling to produce
$-8\pi G\rho/c^2$ when divided by the kinetic prefactor $1/(4\pi G)$:
\begin{equation}
\frac{\text{matter variation}}{1/(4\pi G)} = \frac{8\pi G}{c^2}\,\rho
\quad\Longrightarrow\quad
\text{matter variation} = \frac{2}{c^2}\,\rho.
\end{equation}

So $\mathcal{L}_M = -(2/c^2)\,\psi\,\rho$ gives the correct field equation.

Equivalently, keep the matter coupling as $-(c^2/2)\psi\rho$ and
multiply the kinetic prefactor by $c^4/4$:
\begin{equation}
\frac{a_*^2}{8\pi G} \;\longrightarrow\; \frac{a_*^2\,c^4}{32\pi G}.
\label{eq:corrected-prefactor}
\end{equation}

\textbf{The corrected action is:}
\begin{equation}
\boxed{
S_\psi = \int dt\,d^3x\left\{
  \frac{a_*^2\,c^4}{32\pi G}\,W\!\left(\frac{|\nabla\psi|^2}{a_*^2}\right)
  - \frac{c^2}{2}\,\psi(\rho - \bar\rho)
\right\}.
}
\label{eq:action-corrected}
\end{equation}

\textbf{Verification:} Vary \eqref{eq:action-corrected}:
\begin{align}
\frac{\delta S}{\delta\psi} &= -\frac{a_*^2 c^4}{32\pi G}
  \cdot\frac{2}{a_*^2}\,\nabla\cdot[W'\nabla\psi]
  - \frac{c^2}{2}\rho \nonumber\\
&= -\frac{c^4}{16\pi G}\,\nabla\cdot[\mu\nabla\psi]
  - \frac{c^2}{2}\rho = 0 \nonumber\\
\Rightarrow\;&
\nabla\cdot[\mu\nabla\psi] = -\frac{8\pi G}{c^2}\,\rho. \quad\checkmark
\end{align}

\subsection{Dimensional verification of the corrected action}

\begin{align}
\left[\frac{a_*^2 c^4}{32\pi G}\,W(X)\right]
&= \frac{\text{m}^{-2}\cdot\text{m}^4\text{s}^{-4}}
       {\text{m}^3\text{kg}^{-1}\text{s}^{-2}}
= \text{kg}\,\text{m}^{-1}\,\text{s}^{-2}. \\
\left[\frac{c^2}{2}\psi\rho\right]
&= \text{m}^2\text{s}^{-2}\cdot\text{kg}\,\text{m}^{-3}
= \text{kg}\,\text{m}^{-1}\,\text{s}^{-2}.
\end{align}

Both terms now have the \textbf{same} dimensions (energy density).
The $c^4$ discrepancy identified at i16 is resolved. $\checkmark$

\subsection{Alternative: redefine $a_*$}

Instead of modifying the prefactor, one can redefine:
\begin{equation}
\tilde{a}_* \equiv \frac{2a_0}{c^2} \cdot \frac{c^2}{2} = a_0,
\quad\text{i.e., use $a_0$ directly as the gradient scale,}
\end{equation}
or equivalently absorb $c^2$ factors into the argument. But this changes
the relationship $a_* = 2a_0/c^2$ stated throughout the paper. The
cleanest fix is \eqref{eq:corrected-prefactor}.

\section{Effect on the temporal sector and $c_\psi$}
\label{sec:temporal}
%=============================================================================

The full dynamic action (Eq.~(6)) is:
\begin{equation}
S_\psi = \int dt\,d^3x\;\Biggl\{
  \frac{a_*^2}{8\pi G}\biggl[
    W\!\biggl(\frac{|\nabla\psi|^2}{a_*^2}\biggr)
    + K\!\biggl(\frac{c}{a_0}|\dot\psi - \dot\psi_0|\biggr)
  \biggr]
  - \frac{c^2}{2}\,\psi(\rho - \bar\rho)
\Biggr\}.
\label{eq:action-full-paper}
\end{equation}

The corrected version is:
\begin{equation}
S_\psi = \int dt\,d^3x\;\Biggl\{
  \frac{a_*^2 c^4}{32\pi G}\biggl[
    W\!\biggl(\frac{|\nabla\psi|^2}{a_*^2}\biggr)
    + K\!\biggl(\frac{c}{a_0}|\dot\psi - \dot\psi_0|\biggr)
  \biggr]
  - \frac{c^2}{2}\,\psi(\rho - \bar\rho)
\Biggr\}.
\label{eq:action-full-corrected}
\end{equation}

\subsection{Linearization of the temporal sector}

Define $\Delta \equiv (c/a_0)|\dot\psi - \dot\psi_0|$ (dimensionless).
For small perturbations $\delta\psi$ about a static background with
$\dot\psi_0 = 0$:
\begin{equation}
K(\Delta) \approx \tfrac{1}{2}\Delta^2 = \tfrac{1}{2}\frac{c^2}{a_0^2}\,\dot{\delta\psi}^2.
\end{equation}

The temporal contribution to the action density is:
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{L}_T = \frac{a_*^2 c^4}{32\pi G}\cdot\frac{c^2}{2a_0^2}\,
\dot{\delta\psi}^2.
\end{equation}

Now use $a_* = 2a_0/c^2$, so $a_*^2 = 4a_0^2/c^4$:
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{L}_T = \frac{4a_0^2}{c^4}\cdot\frac{c^4}{32\pi G}
\cdot\frac{c^2}{2a_0^2}\,\dot{\delta\psi}^2
= \frac{c^2}{16\pi G}\,\dot{\delta\psi}^2.
\end{equation}

Varying with respect to $\delta\psi$:
\begin{equation}
\frac{\delta\mathcal{L}_T}{\delta(\delta\psi)}
= -\frac{c^2}{8\pi G}\,\ddot{\delta\psi}
\quad\text{(after integration by parts in $t$)}.
\end{equation}

Dividing by the kinetic prefactor from the spatial sector
($c^4/(16\pi G)$):
\begin{equation}
\text{temporal EOM term} = -\frac{c^2/(8\pi G)}{c^4/(16\pi G)}\,\ddot{\delta\psi}
= -\frac{2}{c^2}\,\ddot{\delta\psi}.
\end{equation}

Combined with the spatial term ($\nabla^2\delta\psi$) and source
($-8\pi G\rho/c^2$):
\begin{equation}
\nabla^2\delta\psi - \frac{2}{c^2}\,\ddot{\delta\psi} = -\frac{8\pi G\rho}{c^2}.
\end{equation}

\textbf{Problem}: This has $2/c^2$ instead of $1/c^2$. The verification
equation in \S2.6 is $\nabla^2\psi - (1/c^2)\ddot\psi = -8\pi G\rho/c^2$.

\subsection{Resolution: the temporal argument needs its own scale}

The factor of 2 arises because $K(\Delta)$ and $W(X)$ share the same
overall prefactor. For the \S2.6 verification equation to emerge, the
temporal sector must carry an independent normalization:
\begin{equation}
\boxed{
S_\psi = \int dt\,d^3x\;\Biggl\{
  \frac{a_*^2 c^4}{32\pi G}\,
    W\!\biggl(\frac{|\nabla\psi|^2}{a_*^2}\biggr)
  + \frac{a_*^2 c^4}{64\pi G}\,
    K\!\biggl(\frac{c}{a_0}|\dot\psi - \dot\psi_0|\biggr)
  - \frac{c^2}{2}\,\psi(\rho - \bar\rho)
\Biggr\}.
}
\label{eq:action-final}
\end{equation}

With the extra factor of $1/2$ on the temporal sector:
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{L}_T = \frac{a_*^2 c^4}{64\pi G}\cdot\frac{c^2}{2a_0^2}\,
\dot{\delta\psi}^2
= \frac{c^2}{32\pi G}\,\dot{\delta\psi}^2.
\end{equation}

Variation and dividing by the spatial prefactor:
\begin{equation}
\text{temporal term} = -\frac{1}{c^2}\,\ddot{\delta\psi}. \quad\checkmark
\end{equation}

This recovers $\nabla^2\psi - (1/c^2)\ddot\psi = -8\pi G\rho/c^2$ exactly.

\subsection{Implication for $c_\psi$}

In the linearized Newtonian regime ($\mu \to 1$, $\Delta \ll 1$):
\begin{equation}
c_\psi = c. \quad\text{(Newtonian regime, high gradient)}
\end{equation}

In the deep-field cosmological regime ($\Delta \gg 1$),
$K'(\Delta) \approx 1$, $K''(\Delta) \approx 0$, the temporal second
variation is suppressed by $K''(\Delta_0) \sim 1/\Delta_0^2 \to 0$.
This gives $c_\psi \to 0$ (dust branch), \textbf{regardless} of the
overall normalization.

The $c_\psi$ contradiction from i14/i15 arose from conflating these two
regimes. With the corrected action:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Lab/Solar}: $c_\psi = c$ (propagation at speed of light)
\item \textbf{Deep-field/Cosmo}: $c_\psi \to 0$ (dust, no propagation)
\item \textbf{Crossover}: $c_\psi^2 = c^2 K''(\Delta_0)/[2\mu(x_0)]$,
  interpolating smoothly between $c$ and $0$
\end{itemize}
