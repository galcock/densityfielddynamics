Density Field Dynamics (DFD)

A scalar optical-metric framework in Euclidean ℝ³ with emergent time. The field ψ sets n(x)=eψ and c₁(x)=c e−ψ, yielding 𝒂=(c²/2)∇ψ for matter and appearing to recover the classical tests (perihelion, deflection, Shapiro, redshift). This theoretical framework proposes laboratory and astrophysical tests to distinguish it from ΛCDM/GR baselines. In a verified nondispersive optical band, the co-located cavity–atom ratio across altitude predicts the clean, falsifiable “slope = 2” signature: ΔR/R ≈ 2 g Δh / c².

Technical Summary

  • Revisits Einstein’s 1911–12 variable light-speed program via a scalar field ψ with energy-density sourcing; aims to recover classical GR test coefficients in the appropriate limits.
  • Optical-metric limit preserves two-way light-speed isotropy by construction.
  • Flat rotation curves and Hubble-tension amplitude may arise from line-of-sight density-gradient bias: δH₀( n̂ ) ∝ ⟨∇lnρ · n̂⟩LOS.
  • Synchronization-free one-way light-speed probe via a co-located cavity/atom ratio at two heights (predicted slope ≈ 2ΔΦ/c² in a nondispersive band).
  • Matter-wave interferometry prediction: T³ phase scaling; ~2×10⁻¹¹ rad at T=1 s under current long-baseline conditions.
  • Laboratory bounds on EM back-reaction coupling: cavity stability suggests |λ−1| ≲ 3×10⁻⁵; intentional tests could reach ~10⁻¹⁴.
  • Sketch of a route to Newton’s constant induced by Standard-Model content within the ψ-medium.
