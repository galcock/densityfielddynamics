# L14: Coleman-Weinberg Coefficient for ψFF̃ — Honest Range of β

**Agent:** L14
**Date:** 2026-04-06
**Task:** Derive the one-loop coefficient c_ψ of the ψFF̃ operator from CP²×S³ dimensional reduction and give an honest range for the predicted birefringence β.

---

## 1. Setup

The operator generated by integrating out charged fermions at one loop is

  L ⊃ (c_ψ / 4) · (ψ / f) · F_{μν} F̃^{μν}

with f a UV scale (f ~ M_P or f ~ f_a from the CP²×S³ compactification). The birefringence rotation angle induced by a homogeneous shift Δψ along a photon's line of sight is

  β = (c_ψ / 2) · (Δψ / f) · (geometric factor O(1)).

In natural units where ψ is dimensionless and we absorb f into the definition of Δψ, this collapses to

  β ≈ (c_ψ / 2) · Δψ.

## 2. One-loop coefficient

For a single Dirac fermion of charge Q_f coupled to ψ through a Yukawa or mass-insertion vertex, the standard CW/triangle result is

  c_f = (α / 2π) · Q_f² · g_{ψf}

where g_{ψf} is the dimensionless ψ-fermion coupling (= 1 for universal mass coupling).

Summing the SM charged spectrum (3 generations, all charged leptons and quarks):

  Σ_f Q_f² = 3·(1) + 3·3·(4/9) + 3·3·(1/9)
            = 3 + 4 + 1
            = 8   (per generation: 1 + 4/3 + 1/3 = 8/3; × 3 gens = 8)

So the SM-fermion-loop contribution is

  c_ψ^(SM) = (α / 2π) · 8 · g_{univ} ≈ (4α/π) · g_{univ}.

With g_{univ} = 1, c_ψ^(SM) ≈ 4α/π ≈ 9.3 × 10⁻³.

## 3. KK tower contribution from CP²×S³

The DFD compactification adds a finite KK tower truncated at k_max set by the condensate scale. Each level contributes a charge-squared factor and a ψ-overlap factor. Schematically,

  c_ψ^(KK) = (α / 2π) · Σ_{k=1}^{k_max} n_k Q_k² I_k

where n_k is the multiplicity at KK level k, I_k is the overlap of the k-th Dirac mode with the ψ zero mode on CP²×S³, and Q_k ∈ {0, ±1} depending on U(1)_EM embedding.

For CP² with c_1 = 3 and S³ with unit winding, a rough level-counting gives Σ n_k Q_k² I_k ∈ [k_max, 3 k_max] depending on whether one assumes democratic overlap (I_k ≈ 1) or suppressed overlap (I_k ~ 1/k).

With k_max ≈ 60 (condensate cutoff used elsewhere in this research program):
- Lower bound (I_k ~ 1/k, harmonic sum): Σ ≈ 3 ln 60 ≈ 12 → c_ψ^(KK) ≈ (α/2π)·12 ≈ 1.4 × 10⁻²
- Central (I_k constant, n_k Q_k² ≈ 1): Σ ≈ 60 → c_ψ^(KK) ≈ (α/2π)·60 ≈ 7.0 × 10⁻²
- Upper bound (I_k ≈ 1, with Chern multiplicity 3): Σ ≈ 180 → c_ψ^(KK) ≈ (α/2π)·180 ≈ 2.1 × 10⁻¹

## 4. Birefringence prediction

With Δψ ≈ 0.27 (the value used in H1–H4), β = (c_ψ/2)·Δψ gives:

| Channel               | c_ψ                      | β (rad)            | β (deg)   |
|-----------------------|--------------------------|--------------------|-----------|
| SM loops only         | 9.3 × 10⁻³               | 1.3 × 10⁻³         | 0.07°     |
| KK lower bound        | 1.4 × 10⁻²               | 1.9 × 10⁻³         | 0.11°     |
| KK central            | 7.0 × 10⁻²               | 9.5 × 10⁻³         | 0.54°     |
| KK upper bound        | 2.1 × 10⁻¹               | 2.8 × 10⁻²         | 1.6°      |
| H1–H4 quoted (α/2)    | 3.6 × 10⁻³               | 4.9 × 10⁻⁴         | 0.028°    |

The Planck/ACT/SPT measurement β_obs ≈ 0.30° ± 0.11° lands inside the **KK central band** but is incompatible with the **α/2 normalization** quoted in H1–H4 (too small by a factor ~10) and with the **SM-only loop** (too small by ~4).

## 5. The "α/2" claim — why it is not rigorously derived

The H1–H4 write-ups presented c_ψ = α/2 as if it were the unique one-loop answer. In fact that value arises only under very specific assumptions:
1. A single universal Yukawa g_{ψf} = 1.
2. A specific field normalization ψ → ψ / (2π f).
3. Neglect of the CP²×S³ KK tower entirely.
4. A factor of π absorbed into the definition of F̃.

Dropping any of these moves c_ψ by up to an order of magnitude. There is no first-principles CW calculation in the DFD corpus that fixes the O(1) prefactor; the 19/3 ≈ 6.3 combinatoric (counting Q² over quarks without multiplying by 3 generations) and the 8 (counting correctly with 3 generations) both get paraphrased as "order unity" but are different at the factor-of-2 level.

## 6. Honest range

Without a rigorous mode-by-mode CW computation on CP²×S³ with specified U(1)_EM embedding and ψ-overlap integrals, the honest DFD prediction for the isotropic cosmic birefringence is

  **β ∈ [0.03°, 1.6°]**,  central value ≈ 0.3°–0.6°.

This range:
- Includes the observed β_obs ≈ 0.30° ± 0.11° comfortably at its lower–central end.
- Excludes the naive "α/2" estimate (0.028°) as too small by a factor of ~10.
- Does not uniquely predict 0.21° — any number in this decade is consistent with current DFD assumptions.
- Is therefore **consistent but not a sharp prediction**. A genuine derivation requires (i) fixing the ψ-overlap integrals I_k on CP²×S³, (ii) specifying how the SM charged fermions sit in the KK tower vs. the zero-mode sector, and (iii) the ψ field normalization convention.

## 7. Verdict

The ψFF̃ mechanism in DFD produces cosmic birefringence at the right order of magnitude (tenths of a degree), but the H1–H4 claim that "β = α/2 × Δψ is a sharp prediction" is **not rigorously supported**. The coefficient is accurate only up to an O(10) uncertainty dominated by the CP²×S³ Dirac-mode overlap integrals, which have not been computed. Agreement with Planck/ACT/SPT should be stated as "consistent with DFD's expected range" rather than "predicted value".

**Required follow-up work** to upgrade this from a consistency check to a prediction:
- Compute the Dirac spectrum and ψ-overlap integrals on CP²×S³ with the actual DFD metric.
- Fix the U(1)_EM embedding in the KK tower (which modes carry electric charge).
- Do the CW integral mode by mode and sum, with a specified cutoff matching the condensate scale.
- Cross-check against the independent "CS topology" channel that gave c_ψ ≈ 45α/π (Route 2 in the H1–H4 notes), which is a factor of ~500 larger and clearly overestimates.

Until that is done, report β = O(0.1°–1°) as the DFD band, with 0.3° as a plausible central value but no claim of sharpness.
