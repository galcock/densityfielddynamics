## 2. The 11D → 4D reduction, step-by-step

### 2a. Starting action
The only strictly gauge-invariant cubic term in 11D with a 3-form potential C₃ is the CS-like term

  S ⊃ (κ/6) ∫_{M₁₁} C₃ ∧ G₄ ∧ G₄,

with G₄ = dC₃. Here κ is fixed by M-theory to κ = 1/(2π)⁴ · 1/(6) in units where ℓ_P = 1 — but the overall prefactor is not what's under dispute; what matters is the ratio to the Maxwell kinetic term that will also come from reduction.

### 2b. The χ ansatz
Writing C₃ = χ(x) · η₃, where η₃ = ω_{S³}/Vol(S³) is a **unit-normalized** harmonic 3-form on S³ (so ∫_{S³} η₃ = 1), the field χ(x) is a 4D scalar (axion-like).

### 2c. 4D gauge fields
The 4D Maxwell field A_μ with field strength F does not emerge from C₃ on CP² × S³ in any direct way. The standard M-theory recipe is that 4D gauge fields come from G₄ on two-cycles of the compactification, i.e. A = ∫_{Σ₂} C₃ with Σ₂ ⊂ CP². But S³ has no 2-cycles and CP² has H₂ = ℤ generated by the Kähler class [ω]. So an abelian A does exist from the CP² side.

Schematically:
  C₃ = χ · η₃ + A ∧ ω_CP² + ...
  G₄ = dχ ∧ η₃ + F ∧ ω_CP² + ...

### 2d. Plugging into CS
  C₃ ∧ G₄ ∧ G₄ ⊃ (χ η₃) ∧ (F ∧ ω_CP²) ∧ (F ∧ ω_CP²)
             = χ · F ∧ F · η₃ ∧ ω_CP² ∧ ω_CP².

Integrating over the internal CP² × S³:
  ∫_{S³} η₃ = 1,
  ∫_{CP²} ω_CP² ∧ ω_CP² = 2  (the Pontryagin/Euler-type intersection number; equivalently ∫ c₁² = 9 for Fubini–Study, or 2 with the normalization that [ω] generates H² and ∫ ω² = 2 for CP² with unit volume).

So the 4D term becomes
  S₄ ⊃ (κ/6) · N_CP² · ∫_{M₄} χ · F ∧ F,
where N_CP² ∈ ℤ is the CP² intersection number (an integer depending on normalization, typically 1, 2, 3, or 9).

### 2e. Canonical normalization
To compare with (β/4π) χ F ∧ F, we need canonical normalizations for both χ and A. The χ kinetic term comes from G₄ ∧ ⋆G₄ reduction:
  ½ (Vol(CP²)/Vol(S³)) · ∂χ ∂χ,

After rescaling χ → χ̂ and A → Â to canonical form, the coefficient of χ̂ F̂ F̃̂ becomes

**Every single volume factor is a free parameter** (set by the radii R_CP² and R_S³). There is **no** step in the reduction where k_max = 60 appears.

## 3. Where does k_max = 60 supposedly enter?

Route 2 as stated in H1-5 invokes k_max = 60 as a "Chern–Simons level." But a CS level only makes sense for a **3D** CS theory written on a closed 3-manifold, where k ∈ ℤ labels the topological class via

  S_3DCS = (k/4π) ∫ Tr(A dA + ⅔ A³).

The 11D term above is a **higher-form** CS-like term; it has no integer "level" k apart from the quantization of G₄-flux
  (1/2π) ∫_{Σ₄} G₄ ∈ ℤ.

The number 60 in H1-5 traces back to a counting of S³ harmonic modes up to some cutoff (the "k_max = 60" of the spectral truncation used elsewhere in the DFD v3.3 program). That is a **completely different** integer from any CS level. Conflating the two is the first red flag.

## 4. Attempting the stated cancellation

The claim is that the k_max factor "drops out" leaving α/2. Let's be concrete. The DFD ψ-screen contribution to birefringence is typically written as β_ψ = (e²/4π) · (something) = α · (something). For the CS contribution one would need

  β_CS = (c · N_CP²) / (something involving k_max),

and then demand c · N_CP² / k_max² · k_max² = α/2, i.e. arrange the numerator and denominator k_max factors to cancel.

But no step of the reduction I did in §2 produces **two** factors of k_max — one comes from the mode count (if one even accepts that identification), and the other would have to come from an *assumed* normalization of χ involving 1/k_max. That second factor is **put in by hand**. There is no geometric reason for it.

Equivalently: the "cancellation" only works if you write

  coefficient = (k_max · something) × (1/k_max · something else)

where both factors are independently adjustable. A true derivation would pin each factor separately.

## 5. Is there a free numerical coefficient?

**Yes — three of them.**

1. **N_CP²:** the intersection integer is 1, 2, 3, or 9 depending on whether one uses primitive H², c₁, 2c₁, or the Fubini–Study normalization. Route 2 picks the one that makes the arithmetic work.

2. **Vol(CP²)/Vol(S³):** a dimensionless ratio of compactification radii. In standard M-theory compactifications, this is fixed by moduli stabilization (fluxes + non-perturbative effects), **not** by the CS term itself. H1-5 does not supply such a stabilization — it simply absorbs the ratio into the final coefficient.

3. **1/(2π) factors:** the reduction produces various (2π)^n; matching to "α/2" requires choosing the canonical form at a specific n. This is the "1/(2π) gives the /2" claim — but the extra factor of 2 in α/2 is not a 2π, it's a 2. There is no place in the reduction where a plain 2 appears from geometry. The 2 must come from either (i) the "/4π" in the 4D CS term vs "/2π" in the Maxwell term, or (ii) a factor of 2 in the intersection number. Both are choices, not derivations.

4. **Identification of α:** α = e²/4πε₀ℏc ≈ 1/137.036 is a **running** coupling, scheme-dependent. Route 2 equates β to α/2 without specifying scheme, scale, or whether α is the on-shell Thomson value. This is a further implicit knob.

## 6. Does k_max = 60 drop out?

**No.** It never enters the geometric reduction in the first place. It is introduced by fiat as a label for a spectral cutoff on S³, then appears and disappears in Route 2's algebra by construction. Writing "x = (60/60) · α/2" is not a derivation that 60 drops out; it's an identity.

## 7. Does the CP² Chern class give the right integer?

The CP² intersection integer exists and is an integer — but it is 2 in the simplest normalization, not 1. So even granting everything else, Route 2 needs a factor of ½ somewhere else to land on α/2. That factor must come from the 4D CS convention /4π vs /2π — which is itself a **convention**, not a derivation.

## 8. The "1/(2π) gives the /2" claim

In 4D, a canonically normalized axion coupling is written

Compared to the DFD "β/4π" notation, g = 8π · β. There is no unique 1/2 that falls out of 2π; depending on whether one uses the 8π² or 32π² or 4π convention, one can recover any rational multiple. Route 2 picks the convention that lands on α/2.

## 9. Final verdict

Route 2 is **numerology**, not a derivation. Specifically:

- The starting 11D CS term is legitimate M-theory, but the reduction does **not** naturally produce an α/2 coefficient.
- The "k_max = 60 drops out" claim conflates two unrelated integers (spectral cutoff vs flux quantum) and then cancels something against itself.
- At least three independent free knobs (CP² intersection convention, Vol ratio, (2π)^n normalization) are tuned to land on α/2.
- α is not even uniquely defined at the compactification scale without specifying a running scheme.

A genuine derivation would:
1. Fix the compactification volumes from moduli stabilization, not absorb them.
2. Use a specific canonical normalization throughout and carry all 2π's honestly.
3. Produce a coefficient that is an unambiguous rational × α, with the rational coming from integer topological data (N_CP², flux quantum) that is computed, not chosen.
4. Show that the RG running from the compactification scale to the optical scale lands on the same α used in the 0.209°/Mpc birefringence prediction.

None of these steps is performed in H1-5 Route 2. The match to α/2 is **retrofit**.
