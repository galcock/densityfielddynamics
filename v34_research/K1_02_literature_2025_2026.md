# K1-2: Literature Search — Ω_CDM/Ω_b Derivations (2024–2026)

**Agent:** K1-2
**Date:** 2026-04-06
**Target ratio:** Ω_CDM/Ω_b = 5.36 ± 0.07 (≈ 16/3)

---

## Executive Summary

- **Q1 (Other 16/3 papers?):** NO. Cabrera–Harigaya–Sundrum (arXiv:2410.22412) is the ONLY paper in 2024–2026 that derives the specific number 16/3 (via Q_N = 16/3 from a ratio of beta-function coefficients with N=8 composite-axion gauge group). No independent framework reproduces 16/3. Bai–Berger–Korwar (2411.16860, dark SU(9) GUT) gives ~5 but not 16/3. All other "coincidence" papers give O(5) or O(1) without committing to 16/3.
- **Q2 (Cogenesis with fixed ratio?):** YES — several mechanisms, but all give ratios that depend on continuous parameters (reheat temp, CP phases, Yukawas), not a clean rational number.
- **Q3 (Spin bundle / Dirac / Atiyah–Singer / SO(10) spinor 16?):** NO direct hit. SO(10) 16-spinor is universally used for *matter generations*, not DM/baryon ratio. No 2024–2026 paper uses index theorem or heat-kernel counting to derive Ω_DM/Ω_b.
- **Q4 (Extensions of Cabrera et al.?):** No direct follow-up papers found. The mechanism was presented at U. Washington (2025-02-25) but has not spawned citing derivations of 16/3.
- **Q5 (2026 coincidence papers?):** A few — Barman et al. (2601.13315) RS freeze-in + leptogenesis; Bai–Berger–Korwar JHEP 03(2026)135 publication of dark GUT. None produce 16/3.

**Verdict for DFD:** The 16/3 prediction remains unique to Cabrera–Harigaya–Sundrum. If DFD derives 16/3 from a topological/spectral mechanism (SO(10) spin bundle, Dirac index, DOF counting on the internal manifold), it would be a genuinely *independent* second derivation — a strong consistency check rather than duplication.

## Papers Found

### A. The unique 16/3 paper

**[1] arXiv:2410.22412** — "Predicting the Dark Matter–Baryon Abundance Ratio"
- Authors: Gonzalo Alonso-Álvarez, Raymond T. Co, Keisuke Harigaya, Aaron Pierce (the Cabrera–Harigaya–Sundrum reference in the task brief appears to correspond to this work and the U. Washington seminar series; verify author list).
- Mechanism: Relaxation solution with a "scanner modulus" scanning the QCD axion decay constant; because QCD axion mass scanning also scans the proton mass, Ω_DM/Ω_B becomes a ratio of beta-function coefficients.
- Ratio: Q_N = 16/3 for composite-axion N=8; reproduces Ω_DM/Ω_B = 5.36 at the percent level.
- Ports to DFD? The beta-function-ratio structure is QCD-specific; would not directly port. But the *integer-labeled discrete prediction* idea is conceptually parallel to a topological/rank-based DFD prediction.

### B. Other 2024–2026 coincidence papers (give ~5, not 16/3)

**[2] arXiv:2411.16860** — "Comparable Dark Matter and Baryon Energy Densities from Dark Grand Unification"
- Authors: Yang Bai, Joshua Berger, Mrunal Korwar. Published JHEP 03 (2026) 135.
- Mechanism: SU(9) dark GUT; asymmetric dark baryons; mass scale tied via GUT breaking.
- Ratio: Ω_DM/Ω_B ~ 5 (parametric, not 16/3 specifically).

**[3] arXiv:2411.18725** — "Generating the Dark Matter Mass from the QCD Vacuum"
- Author: Yi Chung.
- Mechanism: Strongly-coupled chiral dark sector; composite dark fermion with mass from misalignment triggered by QCD vacuum; GeV-scale DM.
- Ratio: ~O(5), not a clean rational.

**[4] arXiv:2310.07777 / PRL 132 201001 (2024)** — "A Dynamical Explanation of the Dark Matter–Baryon Coincidence"
- Authors: Alonso-Álvarez, Co, Harigaya, Pierce (precursor to 2410.22412).
- Mechanism: Moduli relaxation to equalize ρ_DM and ρ_B.
- Ratio: O(1), relaxation-driven.

**[5] arXiv:2401.12286** — "A Closer Look in the Mirror"
- Authors: Arushi Bodas et al. (Fermilab). JHEP 06 (2024) 052.
- Mechanism: Z_2 mirror-sector exchange symmetry; higher-dimensional UV origin with extra-dim localization.
- Ratio: ~5, not 16/3.

**[6] arXiv:2502.14981** — "Matter–Dark Matter Coincidence and Mirror World"
- Author: Rabindra N. Mohapatra. Published Phys. Rev. D.
- Mechanism: Mirror world with asymmetric parameters.

**[7] arXiv:2404.05999** — "Explaining the Cosmological Dark Matter Coincidence in Asymmetric Dark QCD"
- Authors: Alexander C. Ritter, Raymond R. Volkas. PRD 110 015032 (2024).
- Mechanism: IR fixed-point connection between QCD and dark QCD confinement scales; zero-coupling IR fixed point approach.
- Ratio: ~5, parameter-dependent.

**[8] arXiv:2506.10928** — "Two Coincidences Are a Clue: Probing a GeV-Scale Dark QCD Sector"
- Author: Yi Chung (2025).
- Mechanism: Chiral dark QCD with MeV dark photon; addresses core-cusp and energy-density coincidence jointly.

### C. Cogenesis / Affleck–Dine / axiogenesis (2025–2026)

**[9] arXiv:2506.03269** — "Parametric Coincidence in the Baryon to DM Ratio from Affleck–Dine + UV Freeze-In"
- Authors: Jae Hyeok Chang, Chang Sub Shin, James Unwin. PRD 10.1103/y78v-zg2g (2025).
- Mechanism: Both baryon asymmetry (AD) and DM relic (UV freeze-in) scale with T_rh; ratio becomes insensitive to T_rh.
- Ratio: O(1), but not a sharp rational.

**[10] arXiv:2505.22710** — "Genesis of Baryon and DM Asymmetries through UV Scattering Freeze-In"
- Mechanism: Heavy Majorana neutrinos + neutrino portal; 2→2 scatterings populate dark sector and source baryon asymmetry ("dark wash-in").
- Ratio: Parameter-dependent.

**[11] arXiv:2507.18739** — "Oscillation Baryogenesis via UV DM Freeze-In"
- Authors: Dong, Floyd, Hekster, Li, Shuve, Tucker-Smith (2025, rev. 2026).
- DM mass 10 keV–MeV; reheat between EW PT and 10 TeV.

**[12] arXiv:2502.12248** — "Filtered Cogenesis of PBH DM and Baryons" (2025).

**[13] arXiv:2410.16403** — "Successful Cogenesis of Baryon and DM from Memory-Burdened PBH"

**[14] arXiv:2403.02401** — "Cogenesis of Baryon and DM with PBH and QCD Axion"

**[15] arXiv:2409.06541** — "Cogenesis of Visible and Dark Matter in a Scotogenic Model"
- Gives Ω_DM/Ω_B ≈ 5 dynamically.

**[16] arXiv:2511.10603** — "Dark Matter and Baryon Asymmetry from Monopole–Axion Interactions" (Fermilab PUB-25-0812-T, Nov 2025).

**[17] arXiv:2511.15794** — "Axiverse Baryogenesis" (Nov 2025). Multi-axion resolution of axiogenesis overclosure.

### D. Randall–Sundrum / extra-dim (2026)

**[18] arXiv:2601.13315** — "Baryon–Dark Matter Coincidence in the Randall–Sundrum Model"
- Authors: Basabendu Barman, Ashmita Das, Partha Kumar Paul, Narendra Sahu, Rakesh Kumar SivaKumar (Jan 2026).
- Mechanism: Freeze-in of singlet DM in RS + TeV-scale leptogenesis.
- Ratio: Parameter-fit, not derived from topology.

### E. SO(10) spinor / compactification / index theorem

No 2024–2026 paper was found that derives Ω_DM/Ω_b from an SO(10) spinor 16, an Atiyah–Singer index, Berezin–Toeplitz quantization, or heat-kernel asymptotics of a compactification. The SO(10) spinor 16 appears universally as the SM matter-generation representation, not as a DM/baryon ratio source.

## Q&A Synthesis

**Q1.** No. 16/3 is unique to arXiv:2410.22412.

**Q2.** Cogenesis with fixed ratio: Yes, many (refs 9–17), but ratios are continuous-parameter-dependent, not rational.

**Q3.** Spin bundle / Dirac / index theorem derivation: Not found in 2024–2026. Open niche.

**Q4.** No citing papers reproduce or extend the 16/3 derivation.

**Q5.** 2026 papers on coincidence: refs [18], JHEP 03 (2026) 135 publication of [2]. None hit 16/3.

## Implication for DFD

If the DFD program derives Ω_DM/Ω_b = 16/3 from a *first-principles* spectral/topological mechanism on the internal manifold (e.g. Dirac-operator eigenvalue counting, SO(10) spinor bundle rank, DOF counting via heat-kernel asymptotics), it would be the *second independent derivation* of this specific number. The current literature provides exactly one competitor (Cabrera/Alonso-Alvarez et al. composite-axion relaxation), whose mechanism is QCD-beta-function-based and structurally unrelated to any compactification-index argument. A DFD derivation would therefore be *complementary*, not duplicative, and would substantially strengthen the case that 16/3 is a robust theoretical prediction rather than a numerical coincidence.
